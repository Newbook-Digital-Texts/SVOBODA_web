About Us

At heart, the Svoboda Diaries Project is a team research effort combining the work of experienced professionals, skilled graduate students, and a cohort of talented and committed undergraduate research interns. Our interns are engaged in all aspects of our work from from transcribing the original texts to historical research, text markup and programming. The accomplishments of our project would have been impossible without our interns.

Staff

Stacy Waters

Stacy Waters, Ph.D, is an Affiliate Instructor in Near Eastern Language and Civilization and acts as technical consultant for the Newbook Digital Texts Project. He has taught courses in digital humanities, published articles on medieval English texts, as well as providing consulting for technology-based projects at CARTAH (Center for Advanced Research Technology in the Arts and Humanities) at the University of Washington.

Walter Andrews

Walter is the Svoboda Diaries Project PI and Director. He oversees all the work of the project outside of Iraq but depends upon his professional staff and our undergraduate interns for accomplishing the project's core work.

Sarah Ketchley

Sarah Ketchley, Ph.D, is a Lecturer in Near Eastern Languages and Civilization, and is the co-founder of Newbook Digital Texts. She is the Director of the Emma B. Andrews Diary Project, and Digital Project Manager for the Baki Project. She teaches introductory courses in Digital Humanities, and is researching the excavation and exploration of Egypt during the so-called ‘Golden Age’ of Egyptology at the end of the nineteenth and start of the twentieth centuries.
